I am very concerned with her recommendations for the future of Touro's DPT program. She discussed Touro having CE lasting only two weeks. As a 3rd year student, I do not think it is feasible for a student to move, learn, and fully grasp a setting in only two weeks. Additionally, she discussed shortening Touro's program to under 3 years and did not clearly state how this transition would preserve the doctorate title in physical therapy. I'm also concerned with her wanting to choose applicants solely based on their GPA without requiring an essay, interview, or shadowing experiences. Although undergraduate GPA has been shown to improve first time NPTE pass rates; I think an alternative solution would be to raise the minimum science or prerequisite GPA and consider choosing students who have shadowing/work experience, essays, letter of recommendations, and interviews to help select students who have the potential to create well-rounded professionals who are not only academically sucessfull but are also personable, empathatic, and can communicate effectively to provide optimal patient care.
